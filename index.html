How ordering food with a personal AI agent works

1. Tell Super what you want

Message or speak naturally: cuisine, dietary rules, delivery window, or “same as last Tuesday.”

2. Super uses the apps

Super opens food and grocery sites like a human would, navigating menus and addresses with real computer use.

3. Cache the workflow

The steps are stored in a reusable computer-use cache, so repeating the order doesn’t start from zero.

4. You confirm checkout

Super prepares the order and asks for confirmation before anything is finalized.

Why people are turning to AI for food ordering

Major delivery platforms are already experimenting with AI ordering via prompts and photos (cnbc.com, techcrunch.com). Grocery chains like Pick n Pay have launched AI agents to handle orders in any language (mybroadband.co.za).

Analysts note that as ordering shifts from humans to bots, pricing and UX are being redesigned (bain.com), while consumers increasingly expect frictionless ordering (fortune.com).
